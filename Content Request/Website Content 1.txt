
About

Dr Minakshi Dewan Researcher • Author • Academic
Minakshi Dewan is a researcher and writer with a PhD in Social Medicine and Community Health. Her work with International development organisations explores gender, traditional healing, disability, and food systems. Her bestselling debut non-fiction book, The Final Farewell, featured in leading Indian and international publications, has been shortlisted for an award by the Association for the Study of Death and Society (ASDS).
Minakshi’s writing has appeared in The Hindu, Deccan Herald, Devex, The Indian Express and The Telegraph. She has also authored a children’s book with Pratham Books and presented her research at prestigious global forums, including the American Anthropological Association and the Death, Dying and Disposal (DDD17) conference.
Collaborating with international development organisations, award-winning filmmakers, and global production houses, she works to bring powerful human stories to life.

Edited Books
1. 2023: Book - Ethnomedicine and Tribal Healing: Practices in India.
Book Chapter- Ethics, Morality, and Healing: A Bhil Perspective. 
About the Chapter: Health-seeking behaviours in tribal communities often reflect a pattern of medical pluralism. For instance, Bhil, a tribe in Rajasthan, often resorts to traditional healers like herbalists, shamans and dais (midwives) and biomedical practitioners. Though not bound by any written rules/regulations, these practitioners are guided by particular moral/ethical perceptions that govern their behaviour. This chapter looks at various dimensions of ‘ethics’ and ‘morality’ as understood by one of the most deprived sections in the country. It investigates how Bhils characterise different healers as dharmi/adharmi based on the adherence/non-adherence to ethics and moral code. It sheds some light on the factors (other than just economic) essential for Bhils in choosing between different healthcare providers. The public health ramifications of these aspects are also touched upon.
Publisher- Springer Nature

Debut Non-Fiction
October 2023: The Final Farewell: Understanding The Last Rites and Rituals of India’s Major Faiths
Publisher: HarperCollins, India
About: What are the beliefs associated with death in India? How do final rites and rituals reveal the misogyny and caste-based discrimination that plague the country? Who are the people involved in managing the deceased and laying them to rest? What are the economic and environmental costs of saying that final goodbye? Powerful and enlightening, The Final Farewell offers a glimpse into a world that is misunderstood and feared. With compassion and sensitivity, Minakshi Dewan explores the many ways in which some of the country’s major faiths treat the dead: this includes avoidance of human remains, believed by some to be spiritual pollutants; the worship of bodies at the pyre; professional mourners hired to wail loudly for the dead; and musicians devoted to celebrating life at funerals.
Purchase link:

Conferences, Workshops & Consultations
Minakshi Dewan has actively participated in national and international conferences, presenting research and engaging in dialogues at the intersections of death studies, traditional healing, gender, and social anthropology. Her work has been featured at prestigious platforms such as the American Anthropological Association (Tampa), the Death, Dying and Disposal International Conference (Utrecht), and Jawaharlal Nehru University (New Delhi), among others.
Through papers, panel discussions, and invited talks, she brings an ethnographic lens to complex questions of ritual, care, caste, and cultural meanings of death and healing. Her participation in these forums reflects an ongoing commitment to bridging academic research with lived experiences and public understanding.
· Death, Dying and Disposal (DDD17), International Conference
Utrecht, Netherlands | August 2025
Minakshi Dewan presented a paper titled “Caste Dynamics in Last Rites and Rituals” at the Death, Dying and Disposal (DDD17) International Conference in Utrecht, Netherlands. The paper draws from her book, The Final Farewell: Understanding The Last Rites and Rituals of India’s Major Faiths (HarperCollins India), and examines how caste continues to shape practices and access around death rituals in contemporary India.
· Digital Im(mortality): Global Perspectives on Death, Technology, and the Future of Intelligence Leverhulme Centre for the Future of Intelligence, University of Cambridge & Ashoka University | February 2025
Minakshi Dewan participated as one of the experts from the India chapter in the international workshop on Digital Im(mortality), organised by the Leverhulme Centre for the Future of Intelligence, University of Cambridge, and Ashoka University. The workshop brought together experts from India to explore how different cultures interpret digital immortality in relation to local death and afterlife traditions. Participants collaboratively envisioned ethical and culturally rooted futures for AI-driven technologies and digital afterlife practices.
